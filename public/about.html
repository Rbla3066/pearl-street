LED lighting almost sounds too good to be true because it seems to "do it all" by reducing energy costs, virtually eliminating lighting maintenance and improving the quality of lighting. So what are the potential risks with a new LED lighting system? These risks can include the following:


  • You overpaid for the system

  • Forecasted energy savings are not achieved

  • The system and equipment is not reliable and maintenance free as promised

  • The lighting quality is poor (low or uneven light levels, glare, etc.)

  • Light levels decrease and color temperature change unexpectedly over time


An LED lighting system should last 15-20 years or more, and if the project is unsuccessful, you are going to live with the poor results and the recurring complaints from the building occupants for a very long time, and it will be difficult to economically justify a future lighting upgrade (because there just isn’t much more energy left to save).
